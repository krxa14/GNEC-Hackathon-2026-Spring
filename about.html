Locked framing

DoseGuard turns antibiotic instructions into a one-page checklist the patient marks dose by dose until the course is finished.

Antimicrobial resistance is a major global health threat, and WHO identifies misuse and overuse of antimicrobials as major drivers. Published studies show prescription instructions are often misunderstood, especially among low-literacy patients, and pictogram-based materials can improve comprehension. DoseGuard applies that evidence to one narrow dispensing-time workflow: an active checkoff card the patient marks after each dose.

Product scope

DoseGuard is a free web tool any frontline dispenser can use in a few seconds. Enter the antibiotic, doses per day, and total days. The patient leaves with a one-page active checkoff card and marks each dose as they take it.

It does not solve antimicrobial resistance. It addresses one practical misuse pathway at the dispensing counter.

Citation discipline

The cause framing and the product-evidence bridge are cited separately. WHO supports the AMR cause framing. Peer-reviewed studies support the claims about label misunderstanding and pictogram-based comprehension.

  • Cause framing only: WHO fact sheet on antimicrobial resistance
  • Product-evidence bridge: Wolf et al. on patient misinterpretations of prescription drug label instructions
  • Design choice support: Ng et al. on supplementary pharmaceutical pictograms improving comprehension

Wording discipline

DoseGuard uses "active checkoff card" consistently. Product copy avoids unverified claims about offline support and avoids implying WHO identified the dispensing handoff as the failure point.

The patient marks each dose as they take it. The application runs entirely in the browser, uses no accounts, and stores no patient data.
