# ShadowFile Citations

Every citation below maps to a specific feature. Do not use one source to justify multiple unrelated claims.

## 1. Problem framing — SDG 3.4 applied to humanitarian workforce

- World Health Organization. *Mental Health Atlas* 2020 / WHO Health Workforce publications on mental health in humanitarian settings.
- Inter-Agency Standing Committee. *IASC Guidelines on Mental Health and Psychosocial Support in Emergency Settings*, 2007.
- Connorton, E. et al. "Humanitarian relief workers and trauma-related mental illness." *Epidemiologic Reviews* 2012.

Use: supports the claim that humanitarian workforce mental-health burden is real, disproportionate, and under-served. Do not use these to justify the specific UX of ShadowFile.

## 2. Voice / tone — Psychological First Aid

- World Health Organization, War Trauma Foundation, World Vision International. *Psychological First Aid: Guide for Field Workers*, 2011.

Use: supports the "look, listen, link" tone of the offshift check-in and the grounding-without-instruction approach. Do not cite to justify clinical intervention claims.

## 3. Risk recognition — WHO mhGAP-HIG

- World Health Organization, UNHCR. *mhGAP Humanitarian Intervention Guide*, 2015.

Use: supports the recognition-not-diagnosis stance for acute stress, grief, depression, suicide risk. Do not cite to imply ShadowFile delivers mhGAP interventions.

## 4. Screener: suicide risk — Columbia C-SSRS

- Posner, K. et al. "The Columbia–Suicide Severity Rating Scale: Initial Validity and Internal Consistency Findings..." *American Journal of Psychiatry*, 2011.
- Columbia Lighthouse Project. C-SSRS screener, public-use version.

Use: supports the in-app C-SSRS screener items and routing logic. Items reproduced verbatim from the public-use screener.

## 5. Screener: workforce QoL — ProQOL-5

- Stamm, B. H. *Professional Quality of Life: Compassion Satisfaction and Fatigue*, ProQOL.org, 2010.

Use: supports the monthly compassion-satisfaction / burnout / STS screener and subscale cut-offs. Free-use with attribution per ProQOL terms.

## 6. Moral-injury framework

- Shay, J. *Achilles in Vietnam*, 1994. Betrayal-centered moral-injury framing.
- Litz, B. T. et al. "Moral injury and moral repair in war veterans: A preliminary model and intervention strategy." *Clinical Psychology Review*, 2009.

Use: supports the moral-injury walkthrough flow's three-axis naming (perpetration / betrayal / witnessing). Do not cite these to justify any clinical treatment claim.

## 7. ACT / cognitive defusion

- Hayes, S. C., Strosahl, K. D., Wilson, K. G. *Acceptance and Commitment Therapy: The Process and Practice of Mindful Change*, 2011.

Use: supports the defusion phrasing ("I notice the thought that…") as an alternative to disputation. Do not cite to imply ShadowFile delivers ACT.

## Copy guardrails

- WHO PFA → tone only.
- mhGAP-HIG → recognition language only.
- C-SSRS → screener items only.
- ProQOL-5 → screener items only.
- Shay / Litz → moral-injury frame only.
- ACT → phrasing only.

Never collapse these. Never imply WHO or Columbia or Stamm endorses ShadowFile.
